# Writing Case Studies That Recruiters Actually Read

Most case studies fail because they read like a product brochure. Recruiters and hiring managers do not need hype. They need evidence that you can reason through a problem and execute cleanly.

## The structure I prefer

1. Problem
2. Constraints
3. Approach
4. Key decisions
5. Result

## What to avoid

- Vague claims without context
- Fake metrics
- Long intros about the company
- Screenshots without explanation

## What to include instead

- Why the work existed
- What tradeoffs you made
- What you would do differently now
- How the project improved the product or workflow
