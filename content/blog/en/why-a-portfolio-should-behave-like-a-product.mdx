# Why a Portfolio Should Behave Like a Product

A good portfolio is not a stack of screenshots. It is a product with an audience, a job to do, and a measurable outcome: help the right person understand your value fast.

## What that means in practice

- One clear positioning statement.
- One obvious path to the best work.
- Minimal friction to contact you.
- Content that can be scanned in seconds.

## Why this matters for senior roles

Senior hiring is mostly about trust. A portfolio that is vague, noisy, or hard to navigate weakens that trust before the conversation starts.

If your site behaves like an unfocused gallery, it forces the reader to do unnecessary work. If it behaves like a product, it guides them.

## The standard I use

I want a portfolio to answer four things immediately:

1. What do you do?
2. What have you shipped?
3. Why should I trust your judgment?
4. How do I contact you?
